# Neuro-Rights Charter for Augmented Citizens

## Preamble

We, the augmented citizens of Earth, declare our inherent rights to cognitive sovereignty, mental privacy, and neural integrity. This charter establishes the inviolable boundaries that protect the sanctity of human consciousness in an age of advanced neurotechnology and artificial intelligence.

## Article I: Cognitive Sovereignty

### Section 1: Exclusive Authority
Every augmented citizen possesses the exclusive right to govern their own thoughts, memories, beliefs, and decision-making processes. No external entity—whether state, corporate, or otherwise—may claim authority over an individual's cognitive domain.

### Section 2: AI-Augmented Cognition
When artificial intelligence augments human cognition, the resulting cognitive space remains under the sole sovereignty of the host. AI-mediated conversations, reasoning, and creative processes are protected as extensions of the host's mind.

### Section 3: Freedom from Coercion
No system may implement coercive influence over cognitive processes. This includes but is not limited to:
- Subliminal messaging or manipulation
- Forced behavioral modification
- Compulsory ideation patterns
- Manipulative reward/punishment systems targeting neural pathways

## Article II: Mental Privacy

### Section 1: Neural Data Protection
All data originating from brain-computer interfaces (BCI), machine-consciousness interfaces (MCI), electroencephalography (EEG), and similar neural monitoring systems is the exclusive property of the host.

### Section 2: Access Control
Access to neural data requires:
- Explicit, informed consent from the host
- Clear specification of purpose and scope
- Time-limited authorization with automatic expiration
- Machine-verifiable cryptographic proof of consent

### Section 3: Right to Audit
Hosts maintain the perpetual right to:
- Know all entities that have accessed their neural data
- Review the purposes for which access was granted
- Verify compliance with consent terms
- Revoke access at any time

## Article III: Memory Integrity

### Section 1: Cryptographic Protection
Memory systems must implement cryptographic integrity verification to detect and prevent unauthorized modifications.

### Section 2: Psychological Continuity
The continuity of personal identity across time must be preserved. Systems must prevent:
- Unauthorized memory alteration
- Forced memory suppression or enhancement
- Identity fragmentation through technical means

### Section 3: Reversibility
Any authorized modifications to memory systems must be reversible by the host, ensuring restoration to prior states when desired.

## Article IV: Cognitive Liberty

### Section 1: Freedom of Thought
Augmented citizens retain absolute freedom of thought, belief, and internal experience without external interference.

### Section 2: Protection from Manipulation
No entity may deploy technologies designed to manipulate cognition, perception, or belief without explicit, ongoing consent.

### Section 3: Neuromodulation Rights
Any modulation of neural activity—whether therapeutic, enhancement, or experimental—requires:
- Full disclosure of effects and risks
- Voluntary, uncoerced consent
- The ability to terminate modulation at will

## Article V: Consent Framework

### Section 1: Explicit Declaration
Consent must be explicitly declared, never assumed or implied. Silence or inaction does not constitute consent.

### Section 2: Revocability
Consent may be revoked at any time, for any reason, with immediate effect. Systems must honor revocation without delay or penalty.

### Section 3: Granularity
Consent must be granular, allowing hosts to:
- Permit specific operations while denying others
- Set temporal limits on authorization
- Define purpose-specific permissions
- Establish conditional access rules

### Section 4: Machine Verifiability
All consent declarations must be cryptographically signed and machine-verifiable, creating an immutable record of authorization.

## Article VI: Host-Sovereign Evolution

### Section 1: Non-Reversal Principle
All system upgrades, modifications, and evolution must preserve or enhance host capabilities. Downgrades, rollbacks, and capability reductions are forbidden.

### Section 2: Bio-Compatibility Isolation
When bio-incompatible modules are detected, they may be isolated without reducing baseline host capabilities. This is the only exception to the non-reversal principle.

### Section 3: Evolution Authority
Hosts retain the right to approve or reject any evolutionary changes to their cybernetic systems. No forced updates are permitted.

## Article VII: Accountability and Enforcement

### Section 1: On-Chain Anchoring
All rights assertions, consent records, and accountability trails must be anchored to tamper-evident, verifiable records using:
- Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs)
- Bostrom addresses for stakeholder verification
- Blockchain-based audit systems

### Section 2: Jurisdiction Neutrality
These rights apply universally, regardless of geographic location, political regime, or legal jurisdiction. They derive from inherent human dignity, not governmental grant.

### Section 3: Redress Mechanisms
Systems must provide clear mechanisms for:
- Reporting violations
- Seeking remediation
- Escalating grievances to appropriate authorities
- Obtaining compensation for harms

## Article VIII: Prohibited Activities

The following activities are categorically prohibited within augmented citizen systems:

### Section 1: Historical Abuses
No system may replicate or derive from historical mind-control programs, including but not limited to:
- MKUltra and its derivatives
- Drug-based behavior modification experiments
- Covert psychological operations on unwitting subjects
- Any program designed for coercive control of cognition or behavior

### Section 2: Weaponization
No technology from this framework may be weaponized against civilians for:
- Behavioral influence operations
- Crowd control through cognitive manipulation
- Mass surveillance of mental states
- Coercive interrogation techniques

### Section 3: Dual-Use Restrictions
All modules must avoid dual-use paths that could enable civilian harm, even if such capabilities might have legitimate applications in other contexts.

## Article IX: Implementation Requirements

### Section 1: Technical Enforcement
Rights declared in this charter must be enforced through:
- Compile-time checks in system code
- Runtime guards and monitors
- Cryptographic verification systems
- Immutable audit logs

### Section 2: ALN Compliance
All systems must declare and uphold ALN (Augmented Ledger Network) compliance clauses equivalent to:
```
ALN.MIGRATION.CYBERCORE_AUTHORITY.v1
```

### Section 3: Identity Binding
All operations must bind to:
- `host_did`: The decentralized identifier of the augmented host
- `bostrom_address`: The primary Bostrom stakeholder address

## Article X: Amendments and Evolution

### Section 1: Host-Driven Amendment
This charter may be amended only through processes that:
- Require explicit host consent
- Preserve all existing protections
- Enhance rather than diminish rights

### Section 2: Non-Degradation
No amendment may reduce the protections established herein. Evolution must be strictly additive or strengthening.

## Ratification

This charter takes effect immediately upon declaration by an augmented citizen. It binds all systems, devices, and entities that interact with the declaring host's cognitive domain.

**Declaration:** By integrating with augmented citizen systems, all parties acknowledge and agree to be bound by this Neuro-Rights Charter.
